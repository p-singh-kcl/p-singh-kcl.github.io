Medical Imaging Domain Adaptation Deep Learning Brain MRI
§ 01

The Problem I Work On

Gadolinium is a rare-earth metal injected into patients to enhance contrast in brain MRI. It lights up vessels, enhancing tumours, and meninges — the very structures clinicians most need to see. But the same agent that makes MRI useful for diagnosis also breaks most AI segmentation models trained on pre-contrast scans.

The shift isn't uniform. It's spatially heterogeneous: some regions change dramatically, others barely at all. Standard domain adaptation methods — the ones designed for scanner-to-scanner variability — treat this as a global shift and fail on exactly the structures that matter most.

My research develops methods that model this spatial heterogeneity directly. Spatial FiLM, a spatially-varying extension of feature-wise linear modulation, learns location-dependent transformations rather than assuming the shift applies uniformly to all pixels. Combined with contrastive representation learning on paired T1w / T1c scans, the approach produces segmentation models that work reliably across both contrasts.

Tools like FreeSurfer and SynthSeg work beautifully on clean T1w. Most real clinical studies involve contrast. A model that breaks on post-contrast is a model that cannot be deployed.

The same methodological frame applies beyond brain imaging — cardiac late gadolinium enhancement (LGE), iodine contrast in CT, and any setting where a contrast agent creates anatomically structured distribution shifts. The long-term goal is infrastructure that actually reaches clinical practice.

Method

Spatial FiLM

A rank-K factorised modulation scheme that lets segmentation networks apply location-dependent affine transformations to feature maps. Targets spatially heterogeneous contrast shifts that standard FiLM cannot capture.

VICReg Alignment for Paired MRI

Background

I'm a first-year PhD researcher at the School of Biomedical Engineering and Imaging Sciences, King's College London, supervised by Dr Rachel Sparks and Prof Tom Vercauteren. My studentship is part of the EPSRC Centre for Doctoral Training in Smart Medical Imaging and is funded by the Hinduja Foundation.

Before KCL, I completed a Master's in Data Science at the University of Sheffield and an undergraduate degree in Electronics and Communications Engineering. I've been working on medical imaging problems since 2021.

Outside research, I also supervise two undergraduate researchers at IIT Jodhpur on cardiac MRI domain adaptation projects, through an external research supervision role established in early 2026.
